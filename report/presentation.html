The Question

What Are Prediction Markets?

Markets for Beliefs

Prediction markets let people trade contracts on future events. The price is the crowd's probability estimate.

$0.65 = the market thinks there's a 65% chance the event happens.

Polymarket

  • Largest decentralized prediction market
  • $1B+ volume during 2024 election
  • Polygon blockchain, transparent order book
  • Politics, sports, crypto, culture, memes

Our Central Question

Can a multi-agent AI system detect pricing mistakes in prediction markets by analyzing social media sentiment before the market corrects them?

Why This Matters

If prediction markets are efficient, they're powerful forecasting tools. If they're not, there's money on the table. Either answer is valuable.

Dual Search Strategy

Each source performs two searches per contract: keyword extraction search (broad) + full contract title search (specific). This captures both general topic sentiment and bet-specific discussion.

NLP

Ensemble Sentiment Analysis

Sensemble = 0.50·SLLM + 0.25·SVADER + 0.25·STextBlob

Why Three Models?

  • DeepSeek LLM (50%) — Captures context, sarcasm, conditional statements, domain knowledge
  • VADER (25%) — Rule-based lexicon optimized for social media, 7,517 features
  • TextBlob (25%) — Pattern-based polarity, trained on reviews, complementary to VADER

Lexicon models regularize the LLM: when DeepSeek hallucinates extreme sentiment, VADER and TextBlob anchor the score.

Batch Processing Architecture

  • 5 posts per LLM call (80% API cost reduction)
  • Structured JSON output with repair logic
  • Fallback: single-post analysis on batch failure
  • Throughput: ~0.9 seconds per post

Rolling Sentiment Windows

  • 6-hour — Short-term momentum
  • 12-hour — Medium-term trend
  • 24-hour — Baseline sentiment

Each window computes: average score, positive ratio, sentiment velocity (trend).

Algorithms

Four Gap Detection Strategies

Type 1 Sentiment-Probability Mismatch

Pimplied = clamp(Pmarket + S̅ · 0.4, 0.05, 0.95)

Flag when |Pimplied - Pmarket| ≥ 4%. Converts aggregate social sentiment into a probability and compares against market odds.

Type 2 Information Asymmetry

Detects when sentiment has shifted (last 3h vs previous 3h) but market odds haven't moved. Flags delayed information incorporation.

Threshold: sentiment shift ≥ 0.10 with no corresponding odds movement.

Type 3 Historical Pattern Deviation

z = (Pcurrent - P̅historical) / σP

Z-score outlier detection. Flags contracts where current odds are ≥ 1.5 standard deviations from their historical mean.

Type 4 Cross-Market Arbitrage

Searches Kalshi + Manifold Markets for matching contracts. Uses LLM-confirmed semantic matching (conf ≥ 0.6) to avoid false positives.

Threshold: cross-platform edge ≥ 10%.

The Honest Truth

Half the gaps are NHL teams where fans think their team will win but the market correctly prices them at near-zero. The #1 gap (98% confidence) is "Will Jesus Christ return before GTA VI?" That's not alpha. That's a meme.

Key Finding

The Social Media Optimism Bias

What We Expected

Social sentiment reveals information the market hasn't priced yet → sentiment-implied odds diverge from market odds → detectable pricing gap → profit.

What Actually Happened

Social media is structurally biased toward optimism. Fans post about their team winning. Enthusiasts post about their candidate winning. This isn't information — it's selection bias in the data-generating process.

29 of 30 detected gaps = social media is more bullish than the market. The market is right, not wrong.

Market vs. Sentiment-Implied Odds

Points above the diagonal = bullish sentiment bias. The NHL cluster (bottom-left) shows fans consistently overestimating their team's chances.

Analysis

Five Reasons This Doesn't Work

1. Speed Mismatch

Polymarket incorporates information in minutes. Our system runs once per day. By the time we detect a gap, it's been closed for hours.

τdetection ≈ 12-24h >> τmarket ≈ minutes

2. No Info Advantage

We read the same Reddit threads, same news, same tweets as Polymarket traders. No proprietary data = no information edge.

3. Sentiment ≠ Information

Fan enthusiasm ≠ informed probability. Our α = 0.4 mapping is uncalibrated — set by intuition, not backtested.

4. Insufficient Historical Data

Only 8 historical odds records in the database. Z-score pattern detection needs hundreds of data points. Information asymmetry detection needs hours of rolling history. We had neither.

5. Liquidity Constraint

Most gaps were on near-zero liquidity contracts (NHL longshots at 0.1%). Any trade would move the price more than the edge. You can't arb what you can't trade.

Theory

Evidence for Weak-Form Market Efficiency

Kyle's Model (1985)

In transparent order book markets, the rate λ at which market makers learn from informed trading approaches instantaneous. Polymarket's blockchain-based transparency makes this even more extreme — everyone sees every trade.

What Our Data Shows

  • 3 cycles with zero gaps detected (market correctly priced)
  • 1 cycle with 30 gaps, all driven by optimism bias
  • Zero validated predictions across all runs
  • The market was right; our sentiment was wrong

Efficiency Spectrum

Liquid contracts (elections, crypto) Highly Efficient
Mid-tier (sports majors, geopolitics) Moderately Efficient
Long-tail (memes, niche, illiquid) Less Efficient

Inefficiency exists in the long tail, but those markets are too illiquid to trade profitably. The efficiency frontier protects itself.

The Core Problem

We built a beautiful real-time pipeline for a signal we haven't proven exists. The correct order: (1) prove signal in backtest, (2) estimate alpha after costs, (3) then build real-time. We did it backwards — and that's an honest, valuable lesson.

Future Work

Prediction Markets as Equity Signal Features

The Pivot: Don't Trade Polymarket — Use It

Instead of trying to beat prediction markets, use their odds as input features for stock forecasting models. Polymarket prices on policy events encode real-time consensus probabilities that directly affect equity valuations.

Example Features

  • Fed rate cut probability → rate-sensitive stocks
  • Tariff escalation odds → trade-exposed equities
  • Ceasefire probability → defense/energy sectors
  • Election outcome odds → policy-sensitive sectors

Why This Might Work

  • Different market — equity traders don't systematically incorporate prediction market odds
  • Forward-looking — unlike lagging economic indicators
  • Incentive-aligned — unlike surveys or polls
  • Continuously available — 24/7 real-time updating
  • Our pipeline already collects this data

Integration Target: Temporal Fusion Transformers

TFT models handle mixed-frequency inputs natively (daily stock prices + real-time prediction market odds) and provide interpretable attention weights showing which prediction market features drive equity forecasts.

Conclusion

What We Learned

The Architecture Works

8 data sources, 4 AI agents, ensemble NLP, 9-table PostgreSQL schema, FastAPI dashboard — production-grade big data pipeline that successfully processes 32K+ social posts across 625 contracts.

The Signal Doesn't (Yet)

Social media sentiment =/= market-beating information. We discovered the optimism bias problem empirically — and that's a finding worth publishing, not a failure.

Negative Results Are Results

Proving that Polymarket is efficient against daily-frequency sentiment analysis is an empirical contribution to the prediction market literature. Most papers only publish wins.

Key Takeaways

  • Prediction markets price faster than we can detect
  • Sentiment ≠ information (optimism bias is real)
  • Prove the signal before building the system
  • The real value: prediction market odds as equity features
  • Big data infrastructure survives the pivot
