What Are Prediction Markets?

Stock markets for beliefs

The Concept

  • Trade contracts on real-world events, not stocks
  • Price = crowd's probability estimate
  • "Will the Fed cut rates in June?" at $0.65 = 65% chance
  • If you think it's 80%, buy at 65 and profit on resolution

Polymarket

  • Largest prediction market — on Polygon blockchain
  • Blew up during 2024 election: $1B+ volume
  • Covers elections, crypto, sports, meme markets
  • Fully transparent order book (every trade is public)

Our Question

If we throw 8 different data sources at this — social media, news feeds, cross-platform data — and run NLP sentiment analysis on all of it, can we find moments where the crowd is wrong?

Where social media knows something the market hasn't priced yet?

Spoiler: it's harder than it sounds.

Graceful Degradation

Missing sources get skipped automatically. Two sources are broken right now — system handles it cleanly. Rate limiting, exponential backoff, and robots.txt compliance on all scrapers.

Dual Search Strategy

For every contract, we search by extracted keywords AND the full contract title. "Will Trump win Iowa?" searches for both "Trump win Iowa" and the full question. Catches broad and bet-specific conversation.

Five Gap Detection Strategies

Multiple angles on the same question: is the market wrong?

1. Sentiment-Probability Mismatch

Convert sentiment to implied probability (alpha = 0.4). Flag when difference exceeds 4 percentage points.

2. Information Asymmetry

Sentiment shifted in the last 3 hours but market odds haven't moved? Potential lag in price discovery.

3. Historical Pattern Deviation

Z-score analysis: is the current price >1.5 sigma from its own historical mean?

4. Cross-Market Arbitrage

Compare Polymarket vs Kalshi vs Manifold. Semantic inversion detection catches "Will X pass?" vs "Will X fail?" false positives.

5. Volume Spike

New in v2.1. Flags unusual volume patterns signaling incoming movement. +390 lines of detection logic.

Confidence Scoring

Every gap gets a 0-100 score: gap size, data volume, cross-source agreement, source count, contract features. Down-weighted when data is thin.

The Highest "Confidence" Gap

98% confidence: "Will Jesus Christ return before GTA VI?"

One platform prices it at 48%, another at 2%. That's not arbitrage — that's two different groups of people pricing a joke differently.

The Zero

Our backtest_results table has zero rows. Not a single gap checked against what actually happened. Every confidence score is an unverified hypothesis.

The Real Finding: Social Media Optimism Bias

We weren't detecting mispricings. We were detecting human nature.

What's Actually Happening

People post about things they're excited about. Nobody writes passionate Reddit threads about how their team is definitely NOT going to win.

The data-generating process itself is biased. Our system picked up that bias and called it alpha.

The NHL Example

  • 15 Stanley Cup contracts flagged as "gaps"
  • Market says 0.1-0.2% — mathematically correct
  • Fan sentiment implies 5%+ for their team
  • Market was right. Fans are biased.

Five Reasons This Doesn't Work

Being systematic about failure

01
Speed Mismatch
Polymarket moves in minutes. We run once a day. By the time we detect a gap at 8 PM, any real gap was closed at 2 PM.
02
No Information Advantage
We're reading the same Reddit threads, same news articles as the people actually trading. No proprietary data. No edge.
03
Sentiment ≠ Information
1,000 fans saying "Go Sharks!" is not one insider knowing something material. We treated them equally.
04
Insufficient History
8 records in our historical odds table. You can't do meaningful z-score analysis on 8 data points.
05
Liquidity Wall
Even real gaps are on contracts where one trade moves the price more than the edge. The efficiency frontier protects itself.

The Pivot: Prediction Markets as Equity Features

If you can't beat prediction markets — use them

The Insight

Polymarket has real-time probability estimates for events that directly move stock prices: Fed rate cuts, tariffs, regulations.

Most equity models use lagging indicators. Prediction market odds are forward-looking and continuously updating.

The Key Asymmetry

The information gap isn't between us and Polymarket. It's between Polymarket and the stock market. Few equity models incorporate prediction market probabilities as features.

Implementation Path

  • Temporal Fusion Transformer — handles mixed-frequency inputs
  • Daily stock prices + real-time Polymarket odds
  • Attention mechanism reveals which features drive forecasts
  • Interpretable — you can see why

Infrastructure Reuse

Our pipeline already pulls Polymarket odds into a database every cycle. Just pipe it into an equity model. No work wasted.
